Conference on AIoT Applications in Smart Construction:
Sensors, IoT Protocols, Machine Learning, Data Analytics, Robotics, and Cloud Platforms (ASCC 2026)

Conference Description

The ASCC 2026 is dedicated to showcasing the latest advancements and innovative applications of AIoT technologies within the construction sector. As construction projects become increasingly complex and demanding, integrating sensors, IoT protocols, machine learning, robotics, and cloud computing is vital for achieving higher safety standards, operational efficiency, and environmental sustainability. This conference provides a platform for academia, industry, government agencies, and technology providers to exchange ideas, share practical solutions, and present successful case studies demonstrating the transformative power of AIoT in construction.

Core themes include sensor-based structural health monitoring, IoT protocols optimized for construction sites, AI-driven predictive analytics for project management and safety, robotics for automation and site operations, and cloud platforms for real-time data visualization and decision-making. Participants will explore real-world deployment examples, discuss challenges such as interoperability and security, and deliberate on future research directions. Whether developing intelligent monitoring systems, deploying autonomous construction machinery, or integrating digital twins, attendees will gain insights into how AIoT is shaping the future of construction in 2026 and beyond, fostering innovation and sustainable growth.

We invite original research papers, case studies, and technical contributions on topics related to Smart Health and AIoT. Topics of Interest

Technical areas of this special session include, but are not limited to:

  • Sensors and IoT Protocols for Construction Monitoring
  • Machine Learning and Data Analytics for Structural and Environmental Safety
  • Robotics and Autonomous Equipment in Construction
  • Cloud and Edge Computing for Data Management in Construction
  • Predictive Maintenance and Asset Management
  • Digital Twins, AR/VR, and Virtual Modeling in Construction
  • Smart Building and Facility Management Systems
  • AI for Quality Control, Inspection, and Safety Monitoring
  • Cybersecurity and Data Privacy in Construction IoT Networks
  • Standards, Challenges, and Future Directions in Construction AIoT

While these topics provide a broad overview of current interests, we welcome submissions that explore innovative ideas, emerging technologies, and interdisciplinary approaches. The scope is intentionally flexible to accommodate the rapid evolution of AI and IoT in medical education. We encourage proposals that push the boundaries of existing knowledge, address new challenges, or propose novel solutions that may not yet be widely explored but hold significant potential for transforming healthcare training in the digital age.
